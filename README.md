# 📊 AtliQ Grands – Hospitality Revenue Analysis (Power BI)

🔎 Project Overview

AtliQ Grands is a 20-year-old luxury hotel chain operating across major Indian cities.  
Due to increased competition and ineffective strategic decision-making, the company has been losing market share and revenue in the luxury/business hotel segment.

This project leverages **Business Intelligence (Power BI)** to analyze historical booking data and uncover revenue leakage, operational inefficiencies, and strategic gaps impacting overall performance.

 🎯 Business Objective

The primary objective of this analysis is to:

- Identify causes of revenue decline
- Detect revenue leakage
- Analyze booking platform dependency
- Improve occupancy and RevPAR
- Provide data-driven strategic recommendations

---

📊 Key Business Insights

 1️⃣ Revenue Leakage
- ₹424M revenue lost due to cancellations
- 25% cancellation rate (1 in 4 bookings cancelled)
- Realization rate ~70%
- 6,759 no-show bookings

➡️ Major revenue drain caused by weak cancellation controls.

---

 2️⃣ Occupancy vs Pricing Gap
- ADR: ₹11.3K (strong pricing power)
- Occupancy: 57.6% (below 70–75% industry benchmark)

➡️ Indicates pricing-demand mismatch and inefficient revenue management.

---

 3️⃣ OTA Dependency Risk
- 40% revenue from a single platform (ABC.com)
- ₹174M cancellation loss from the same platform

➡️ High revenue concentration and commission dependency risk.

---

 4️⃣ City-Level Performance Inefficiency
- Mumbai leads in RevPAR
- Hyderabad significantly underperforms

➡️ Regional strategic imbalance and potential demand issues.

---

5️⃣ Weekday Demand Weakness
- Weekend RevPAR consistently higher than weekday RevPAR

➡️ Indicates weak corporate partnerships and business travel demand.

---

 6️⃣ Customer Experience Gap
- Average rating: 3.62

For a luxury chain, this directly impacts:
- Booking conversion
- Repeat customers
- Pricing power

---

 📈 Strategic Recommendations

- Implement stricter cancellation and partial prepayment policies
- Increase direct booking share to reduce OTA dependency
- Introduce dynamic pricing strategies
- Improve guest experience to achieve 4.2+ ratings
- Strengthen corporate partnerships to improve weekday occupancy
- Apply controlled overbooking strategy to offset no-shows

  📌 Key Metrics Explained

- **ADR (Average Daily Rate)** → Revenue earned per sold room  
- **RevPAR (Revenue per Available Room)** → Revenue generated per available room  
- **Occupancy %** → Percentage of rooms occupied  
- **Realization %** → Successful stays / Total bookings  
- **DBRN** → Daily Booked Room Nights  
- **DSRN** → Daily Sellable Room Nights  
- **DURN** → Daily Used Room Nights
